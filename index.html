The emus proved to be formidable opponents, using their speed (up to 50 km/h) and intelligence to outmaneuver the military forces. They would scatter when under fire and regroup elsewhere.

The wheat fields of Western Australia became the unlikely battlefield. The emus were destroying crops that were meant to help Australia recover from the Great Depression.

The Lewis machine guns, effective in WWI, proved inadequate against the mobile emu forces. Frequent jams and the birds' ability to absorb multiple hits made them nearly useless.

ğŸ“°

The international press had a field day with Australia's military defeat by birds. Headlines around the world mocked the unsuccessful campaign, making it a source of national embarrassment.

Today, the Great Emu War is remembered as one of history's most unusual military campaigns. It has inspired countless memes, documentaries, and remains a beloved piece of Australian folklore.
