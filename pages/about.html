About the Great Emu War

Discover the fascinating historical context behind Australia's most unusual military campaign and learn about our interactive educational project.

Historical Context

The Post-WWI Setting

Following World War I, the Australian government established a soldier settlement scheme to help returning veterans transition to civilian life. These brave men were granted land in Western Australia to establish wheat farms, contributing to the nation's agricultural development.

The Emu Problem

However, the Great Depression had severely impacted wheat prices, making farming already difficult. To make matters worse, approximately 20,000 emus began migrating through the farming areas, destroying crops that were essential for the farmers' survival and Australia's economy.

The Military Solution

Desperate farmers petitioned the government for help. In an unprecedented decision, the military was deployed with machine guns to cull the emu population. What seemed like a straightforward operation became one of history's most unusual military campaigns.

Key Dates

  • October 1932: Emu problem reaches crisis point
  • November 2, 1932: Military operation begins
  • November 8, 1932: First withdrawal and resumption
  • December 10, 1932: Final military withdrawal

Military Forces

  • Commander: Major G.P.W. Meredith
  • Personnel: 2 soldiers + 1 officer
  • Equipment: 2 Lewis machine guns
  • Ammunition: 10,000 rounds

The Opposition

  • Numbers: ~20,000 emus
  • Speed: Up to 50 km/h (31 mph)
  • Tactics: Scattering and regrouping
  • Advantage: Superior mobility and numbers

About This Project

Educational Mission

This interactive website was created to make history engaging and accessible. By combining factual information with interactive elements like our emu game, we aim to help people learn about this unique historical event in a memorable and entertaining way.

Interactive Learning

Our game allows you to experience the conflict from the emus' perspective, helping you understand why they were so successful against the military forces. This hands-on approach makes the historical lessons more impactful and memorable.

Historical Accuracy

While we've added interactive elements for engagement, all historical facts and figures are researched and accurate. Our timeline, statistics, and contextual information are based on historical records and scholarly sources.
